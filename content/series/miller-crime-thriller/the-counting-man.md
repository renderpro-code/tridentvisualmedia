**He counted everything. He never once counted himself.**

Robert Adkins walks into every room and takes inventory. The people. The exits. The small wrong details most people never notice. It's made him a brilliant film producer, a careful husband, and an invisible criminal.

Then Detective K. Miller arrests him in a mall food court, in front of his wife and children, and the counting begins to unravel.

*The Counting Man* moves between Robert's meticulous unraveling and Miller's relentless reconstruction, building toward a reckoning neither of them fully anticipated. A four-point-seven-million-dollar crypto theft. An affair that didn't stay contained. And a man who could calculate every variable except the one that mattered most.

**For fans of Tana French and Harlan Coben** — a literary crime thriller about the stories brilliant people tell themselves, and the people left behind when those stories collapse.
